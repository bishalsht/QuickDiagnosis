SCABIES
Intro:
Scabies is a contagious skin infection caused by the mite Sarcoptes scabiei. The tiny mite burrows itself under the host's skin and causes intense allergic itching. Scabies is classified by the WHO as a water related disease. Most oftenly, this disease is transmitted by direct skin-to-skin contact with a higher risk with prolonged contact.

Signs and Symptoms:
Itching
Rashes appears usually around hands, feet, wrists, elbows, back, buttocks and external genitals.

Cause:
Close physical contact with an infected person
Consumption of unpurified water

Treatment:

Scabies treatment involves eliminating the infestation with medications.
Several creams and lotions are available. You usually apply the medication over all your body, from your neck down, and leave the medication on for at least eight hours.
A second treatment is needed if new burrows and rash appear.

Drugs:
Permethrin 5 perecent
Lindane
Crotamiton(Eurax)
