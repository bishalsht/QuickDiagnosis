MALARIA
Intro:
Malaria is a mosquito-borne infectious disease of humans and other animals caused by parasitic protozoans (a type of unicellular microorganism) of the genus Plasmodium. Commonly, this disease is transmitted via a bite from an infected female Anopheles mosquito, which introduces the organisms from its saliva into a person's circulatory system.

Signs and Symptoms:
Headache
High fever
Back Pain
Shaking Chills
Profuse sweating
Dry cough
Spleen Enlargement
Nausea
Vomiting
Diarrhea

Precautions:
Using insecticide periodically on your surroundings to kill mosquitoes.
Sleeping under a net.
Using mosquito-repellent lotions on your body
Covering your skin

Treatments:
Uncomplicated Malaria may be treated with oral medications.
Recommended treatment for severe malaria is the intravenous use of antimalarial drugs.


Drugs:
Chloroquine
Quinine Sulfate
Hydroxychloroquine
Mefloquine
Atovaquone and Proguanil
